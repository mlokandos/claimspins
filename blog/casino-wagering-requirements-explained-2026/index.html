What Are Wagering Requirements and How Do They Work?

Wagering requirements, also called playthrough conditions, define how many times you must bet your bonus amount before you can withdraw any winnings derived from it. When a casino offers a “100% up to €300 with 35x wagering,” the 35x multiplier applies to the bonus you receive. If you deposit €100 and receive a €100 bonus, you must place €3,500 in total bets (€100 × 35) before your bonus winnings become withdrawable.

It's critical to understand whether the wagering applies to the bonus amount alone or to the bonus plus your deposit combined. Most European-facing casinos in 2026 apply wagering to the bonus only, but some operators calculate it on the total of deposit plus bonus. A €100 deposit with a €100 bonus at 35x wagering means €3,500 in the first scenario but €7,000 in the second. Always check this detail in the terms and conditions before claiming any offer.

The wagering clock starts ticking the moment you activate a bonus. Most casinos give you between 7 and 30 days to complete the playthrough requirement. Any remaining bonus funds and associated winnings are typically forfeited once this window expires. Time pressure is a deliberate design choice, so plan your play sessions accordingly and never claim a bonus right before a busy week when you won't have time to play.

Not every game contributes equally to clearing wagering. Slots usually count 100%, meaning every euro you bet on a slot machine counts in full toward your playthrough target. Table games like blackjack and roulette often contribute only 10% to 20%, which means a €10 blackjack bet might only count as €1 or €2 of progress. Live dealer games may contribute even less or be excluded entirely.

Hidden Wagering Traps Every Player Should Know

Even well-informed players can stumble into wagering pitfalls that void their bonuses or dramatically reduce their chances of cashing out. These are the traps that casinos don't highlight in their promotional banners.

Maximum bet limits during active wagering are the most common bonus killer. Nearly every casino imposes a cap, typically between €5 and €10 per spin, while you're clearing wagering requirements. Place a single bet above this threshold and the operator has the contractual right to confiscate your entire bonus balance and accumulated winnings. This rule applies even if the oversized bet was accidental. Always set your bet size before activating a bonus and resist the temptation to increase it during a hot streak.

Maximum withdrawal caps on bonus winnings catch many players by surprise. Some casinos limit how much you can actually withdraw from bonus-derived winnings, regardless of how much you've won. A €500 bonus might generate €2,000 in winnings, but if the maximum cashout is capped at €1,000, the remaining €1,000 is forfeit. This limit varies widely across operators and is often buried deep in the terms. The five casinos we recommend above have transparent and reasonable withdrawal limits.

Game weighting inconsistencies create confusion and wasted progress. A casino might advertise “play any slot” in its bonus promotion while the fine print excludes specific high-RTP titles from wagering contribution. Popular games like Blood Suckers, Mega Joker, and 1429 Uncharted Seas are frequently excluded due to their exceptionally high return rates. Always cross-reference the excluded games list with your planned slot choices before you start wagering.

Deposit method restrictions can prevent you from claiming a bonus entirely. Some offers exclude deposits made via e-wallets like Skrill and Neteller, while others restrict cryptocurrency deposits. Even if you successfully deposit and claim the bonus, using a restricted payment method may void the entire promotion retroactively. Verify that your preferred deposit method qualifies before transferring any funds.

Currency conversion fees silently eat into your bonus value when playing in a currency different from the casino's base currency. A 2% to 4% conversion fee applied to every transaction adds up quickly over thousands of wagering spins. Whenever possible, choose a casino that supports your local currency natively to avoid this hidden cost.
