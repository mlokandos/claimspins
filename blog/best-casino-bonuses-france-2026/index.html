How to Choose the Right Casino Bonus in France

Not all casino bonuses are created equal, and the headline figure — "100% up to €500" — rarely tells the full story. French players in particular need to evaluate several factors before committing a deposit to any offer. Here is what to look at before you click "Claim":

Wagering Requirements

This is the single most important number in any bonus offer. Wagering (also called playthrough) is the amount you must bet before any bonus winnings can be withdrawn. A 35x wagering requirement on a €100 bonus means you need to wager €3,500 before cashing out. On our list, SpinIt's 30x requirement is the most player-friendly, while Casinia's 40x is slightly above average — though Casinia's larger maximum bonus can offset this for high depositors.

Game Contribution Rates

Most casinos only count a fraction of bets on certain games toward wagering. Slots typically contribute 100%, while roulette and blackjack may contribute as little as 10% or even 0%. If you primarily play table games, a bonus with a high wagering requirement will be much harder to clear. Always check the full terms before claiming.

Bonus Validity Period

Every bonus expires. Standard validity is 30 days from activation, though SpinIt sets theirs at 21 days. If you are a casual player who logs in a few times per week rather than daily, a shorter validity window significantly reduces your chances of clearing the requirement. Choose a casino whose validity window suits your play frequency.

Pro Tip for French Players Always opt in or activate the bonus before making your deposit — not after. Most casinos require you to tick a box or enter a promo code during the deposit process. Making a deposit without activating the bonus first means you forfeit the offer, and most support teams cannot retroactively apply it.

Understanding Wagering Requirements: A Practical Guide

Wagering requirements are the most misunderstood aspect of online casino bonuses. Many players claim a bonus without understanding how much they actually need to wager — and then feel frustrated when they cannot withdraw immediately. Here is a clear breakdown to help you plan before you play.

Deposit + Bonus vs Bonus Only

Some casinos calculate wagering on the sum of your deposit plus your bonus. Others apply the multiplier only to the bonus amount. This distinction matters enormously. A 35x requirement on bonus-only means wagering €3,500 on a €100 bonus. The same 35x on deposit + bonus means wagering €7,000 if you deposited €100 and received a €100 bonus. All five casinos in our list apply wagering to the bonus amount only, which is the fairer calculation.
