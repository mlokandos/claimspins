Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced players fall into traps that drain their free spin value. Being aware of these pitfalls saves you frustration and money in the long run. Here are the most common mistakes we see players make in 2026.

Ignoring wagering requirements. The biggest mistake is treating free spins like free money. A 100 free spins bonus at €0.10 per spin gives you €10 in bonus play. With 35x wagering, you need to bet €350 before you can withdraw any winnings. Understanding this math upfront prevents disappointment later.

Playing excluded games. Some casinos exclude table games, live dealer games, or even certain slot titles from bonus wagering contribution. Every spin on an excluded game is wasted progress. Check the terms, bookmark the eligible games list, and stay within those boundaries until wagering is complete.

Exceeding maximum bet limits. Most bonuses have a maximum bet size during active wagering, typically €5 per spin. Place a €6 bet and the casino can void your entire bonus and any accumulated winnings. This rule exists at virtually every licensed operator and is strictly enforced.

Chasing losses with real funds. Free spins that don't convert to a withdrawal are not losses. They cost you nothing. The real danger begins when players deposit additional funds to chase a bonus that's already gone. Treat every bonus round as an independent event. If the spins run dry, wait for the next promotion.

Skipping the terms entirely. We cannot stress this enough: five minutes of reading bonus terms saves hours of frustration. Look specifically for the wagering multiplier, maximum cashout from bonus winnings, eligible games, time limit, and maximum bet during wagering. These five data points tell you everything you need to know about whether a bonus is worth claiming.
